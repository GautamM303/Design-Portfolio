Original Position Statement

Engineering Design is a process of slowly building "brick by brick" to achieve a certain goal, whether this be for entertainment, or to better humanity. With each problem there are various ways to approach the solution, but in the end there is one optimal way to solve it.

Updated Position Statement

Engineering Design is the iterative process of working towards a solution, with various levels of diverging and converging. With each unique opinion comes a new approach to a solution, and this can be extremely beneficial when observing the entire design space for a solution. Through various methods of validation and verification we can eventually converge onto one optimal solution.

My Values and Beliefs in Engineering

I believe thoughtful design can create meaningful impact—whether it's improving daily experiences or supporting niche communities. I value empathy-driven design, where understanding user needs is the foundation of effective solutions.

In Praxis II, a meeting with the Toronto Bonsai Community reshaped our project. Josh shared how traditional moisture sensors damaged roots and gave inaccurate readings. This led us to pivot toward a non-invasive, Juniper-specific solution grounded in real feedback.

I also believe collaboration drives innovation. In Praxis II, we used pairwise comparison to debate and evaluate early concepts. These structured discussions helped us converge on a stronger design. In Praxis I, Amy and I co-designed a portable chair stabilizer—a simple, practical fix based on student needs.

Adaptability is essential to my approach. When our Arduino-based moisture sensor failed in testing, we pivoted to a phone-based solution that was simpler and more reliable. In CIV102, we redesigned our bridge over five iterations, using MATLAB simulations and feedback to refine structural performance.

Finally, I value accessibility in engineering. Across all projects, I prioritized usability and clarity—ensuring solutions were easy to use, easy to build, and genuinely helpful to those they were meant for.
