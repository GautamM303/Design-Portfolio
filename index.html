Reflections from Studio

Engineering is: The process of creating a product that is beneficial to a group of stakeholders and has undergone thoughtful iteration. Why I think this is: As engineers, we must create the best possible solution by prioritizing functionality and solving problems—often over consumer ease.
Design is: The process of creating a product that is either aesthetically pleasing or user-friendly and intuitive to use. Why I think this: When designing, we must ensure it’s appealing for the user while being realistic and applicable in the real world.
Engineering Design is: A combination of engineering and design—a product that solves the problem efficiently while also being usable and appealing. Why I think this: In decision-making, engineering students must account for multiple DFx factors. It’s easy to over-focus on one area, which can compromise others.
How has framing and Praxis affected your work? Framing has helped me center stakeholder needs. With real clients, I’ve learned how to gather insights respectfully and reframe opportunities into actionable design solutions.
